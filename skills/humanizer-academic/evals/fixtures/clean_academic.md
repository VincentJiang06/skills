Real GDP grew 6.4% in 2021, then contracted 3.5% the following year. The reversal
was not cyclical noise. A local outbreak and tighter global financial conditions
hit a small, open economy at once, and consumption fell with investment. By 2023
the picture had changed: services led, private consumption rose 7.4%, and arrivals
recovered to 34 million. The recovery was real but narrow. Goods exports stayed
soft, which is why the rebound concentrated in travel and face-to-face services
rather than across the board.
