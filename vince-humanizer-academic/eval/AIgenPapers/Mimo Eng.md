# Hong Kong's Economic Development: Retrospect and Prospect (2021-2030)

## Abstract

This paper systematically reviews the economic development trajectory of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region (HKSAR) from 2021 to 2025, providing an in-depth analysis of its growth drivers, structural challenges, and key policy responses. Research indicates that after experiencing the dual shocks of social incidents and the global pandemic, Hong Kong's economy embarked on a recovery path in 2021 and entered a more stable growth track post-2023. Its traditional status as an international financial, trade, and shipping center has been consolidated through national support and self-adjustment, while initial developments have been achieved in emerging sectors like innovation and technology. The paper further conducts a forward-looking exploration of the development prospects, potential growth paths, major risks, and strategic directions for Hong Kong's economy from 2026 to 2030, based on existing plans and macro trends. The study concludes that over the next five years, Hong Kong will continue to evolve under the multiple logics of integrating into national development, promoting industrial upgrading, and maintaining its unique advantages. Its long-term prosperity and stability depend on successful transformation and the synergy of internal and external environments.

**Keywords**: Hong Kong Economy; Economic Development; 14th Five-Year Plan; Guangdong-Hong Kong-Macao Greater Bay Area; International Financial Center; Innovation and Technology

## Part I: Retrospective on Economic Development (2021-2025)

### 1.1 Overall Economic Performance and Cyclical Fluctuations

Over the five years from 2021 to 2025, Hong Kong's economy traced a "V-shaped" recovery trajectory characterized by deep recession, volatile recovery, and subsequent stabilization. The process profoundly reflects the complex interaction between internal and external shocks and policy responses.

- **2021: Recovery Initiation and Uneven Growth**. After a significant contraction in 2020 due to the lingering effects of social incidents and the global pandemic, Hong Kong's economy rebounded strongly in 2021, with real GDP growing by 6.4%. Growth was primarily driven by the robust global demand for goods in the first half of the year, which significantly boosted Hong Kong's goods exports. However, the recovery was notably uneven: industries closely linked to external trade recovered faster, while sectors heavily reliant on people flows, such as tourism, retail, and catering, remained deeply distressed. The number of inbound tourists for the entire year was only about 90,000, virtually zero compared to nearly 56 million in pre-pandemic 2019, continuing to exert pressure on related industry chains.
    
- **2022: Growth Moderation Amid Multiple Challenges**. In 2022, Hong Kong's economy faced a more complex environment. The outbreak of the fifth wave of the pandemic at the beginning of the year severely disrupted local consumption and economic activity. Concurrently, major global central banks entered an aggressive interest rate hiking cycle to combat inflation, suppressing external demand and intensifying financial market volatility. Rising geopolitical tensions, particularly uncertainties in China-U.S. relations, posed potential challenges to Hong Kong's role as an international business hub. Against this backdrop, annual economic growth slowed to 3.2%. Although economic activity recovered somewhat in the second half of the year as anti-pandemic measures were relaxed, the number of inbound tourists for the whole year remained low, constraining the momentum of economic recovery.
    
- **2023-2025: Moving Towards Solid Recovery and Structural Fine-tuning**. 2023 was a pivotal year for Hong Kong's full return to normalcy. The full resumption of travel with the Mainland and internationally at the beginning of the year became the most significant growth catalyst. The number of inbound tourists rebounded sharply to approximately 34 million. Although not yet reaching pre-pandemic levels, this significantly boosted tourism, hotels, retail, and catering. While the financial market faced challenges such as globally high interest rates and weak capital market financing activities, the banking sector remained robust overall, with business segments like Renminbi (RMB) business and wealth management demonstrating resilience. In 2024 and 2025, the economy is expected to maintain moderate growth, with growth drivers becoming more diversified. The government's ongoing initiatives to attract enterprises and talent have begun to show results, partially offsetting the impact of fluctuating external demand. During this period, economic growth is projected to hover within the 2.5%-3.5% range. The economic structure shows more pronounced signs of transformation towards innovation and technology, green finance, etc., while consolidating traditional strengths.
    

### 1.2 Key Driving Factors and Evolution of Pillar Industries

Over the past five years, the engines of Hong Kong's economy have presented a pattern of "traditional pillars under pressure to transform, emerging sectors boosted by policy."

- **Financial Services: Stability with Change, Connecting Inside and Outside**. As the lifeblood of Hong Kong's economy, the financial sector has weathered tests from dramatic shifts in the interest rate environment, rising geopolitical risks, and capital market volatility over the past five years, yet its core position remains solid.
    
    - **Stock Market**: Initial Public Offering (IPO) activities have fluctuated significantly with global market sentiment. However, the continuous expansion and optimization of Hong Kong Exchanges and Clearing Limited's (HKEX) Stock Connect mechanisms (e.g., expanding沪深港通, launching Swap Connect, considering RMB counters) have strengthened its role as a super-connector linking the Mainland of China with international markets.
    - **Asset and Wealth Management**: Hong Kong continues to benefit from the spillover of Mainland wealth growth and global asset allocation needs. Family office business and the Wealth Management Connect have become development priorities. Despite competition from places like Singapore, Hong Kong retains unique advantages in market scale, legal system, and connectivity with the Mainland.
    - **Fintech and Green Finance**: The Hong Kong Monetary Authority (HKMA) actively promotes fintech development, with virtual banks and digital payments becoming more widespread. Green and sustainable finance has developed rapidly, with Hong Kong becoming one of Asia's leading green bond issuance centers, and continuous innovation in related financial products.
    - **Offshore RMB Business**: Hong Kong's position as the world's largest offshore RMB business hub has been further consolidated, with steady expansion in RMB deposits, loans, foreign exchange transactions, and clearing business.
- **Trade and Logistics: Challenges and Opportunities amid Supply Chain Restructuring**. Hong Kong's status as a global trade center faces challenges from regionalization of global supply chains, rising protectionism, and geopolitical factors.
    
    - **Goods Trade**: Total goods trade volume fluctuates with global cycles. Hong Kong retains advantages in re-export trade of high-end consumer goods and electronic products but faces pressure from direct competition from Mainland ports and supply chain adjustments.
    - **Trade-Related Professional Services**: Professional services related to trade, such as arbitration, testing and certification, and supply chain management, maintain strong competitiveness.
    - **Shipping and Air Logistics**: Hong Kong International Airport's cargo volume has long ranked among the world's top. The completion of the Three-Runway System in 2024 will significantly increase capacity. However, the passenger aviation sector's recovery pace is constrained by the extent of tourism demand recovery and regional competition.
- **Tourism and Retail: A Long Thaw from Deep Freeze**. This was the industry most directly and severely impacted by the pandemic.
    
    - **Changes in Visitor Composition**: After reopening, Mainland tourists remain the absolute majority, but their consumption patterns have become more rational, with increased interest in in-depth and cultural experience tours. The singular appeal of the "shopping paradise" has relatively diminished. Growth in short-haul markets like Southeast Asia has become a new highlight.
    - **Retail Format Transformation**: Retail sales rebounded significantly in 2023, but growth was uneven. Traditional strong categories like luxury goods and cosmetics recovered faster, while growth in mass consumer products was moderate. The industry is accelerating online-merge-offline (OMO) integration and developing cross-border e-commerce to adapt to new consumption habits.
- **Innovation and Technology: An Emerging Growth Pole Driven by Policy**. The current government has elevated the development of innovation and technology (I&T) to a strategic height, establishing the Office for Attracting Strategic Enterprises (OASES), launching the "Research, Academic and Industry Sectors One-plus Scheme" (RAISe+), and developing the Hetao Shenzhen-Hong Kong Science and Technology Innovation Co-operation Zone.
    
    - **Life and Health Technology**: This has become a key development area, attracting several world-leading biopharmaceutical and medical device companies to establish or expand their R&D presence in Hong Kong.
    - **Artificial Intelligence and Data Science**: Related research and applications are gradually advancing, but the scale and pace of industrialization still need improvement.
    - **Start-up Ecosystem**: The number of start-ups in Hong Kong continues to grow. Although venture capital activity has been affected by the tightening global financing environment, attention on Deep Tech fields has increased under the guidance of government funds. However, the I&T industry still faces challenges such as limited local market scale, fierce competition for top talent, and the need to improve pathways for commercializing research results.

### 1.3 Major Challenges and Structural Contradictions

Development over the past five years has highlighted deep-seated structural challenges in Hong Kong's economy.

- **High Uncertainty of the External Environment**: Sluggish global growth, divergent monetary policies among major economies, ongoing geopolitical conflicts (e.g., the Russia-Ukraine war), and the long-term nature of China-U.S. strategic competition continuously impact Hong Kong, a highly open small economy, through trade, finance, and confidence channels.
    
- **Risks of Economic Structural Simplification**: Despite government efforts to diversify the economy, finance, trade logistics, professional services, and tourism retail still account for the vast majority of GDP. The economy remains highly dependent on a few industries and the external environment. When these industries face cyclical or structural downturns, the overall economic resilience is insufficient.
    
- **Tension between Social Livelihood and Economic Development**: Deep-seated social contradictions such as the housing problem, wealth gap, and limited upward mobility for youth have not been completely resolved by economic recovery. High business and living costs affect the city's long-term attractiveness to talent and enterprises. Ensuring that the fruits of economic growth benefit citizens across all strata more equitably is key to maintaining long-term social stability and sustained economic prosperity.
    
- **Intensified Regional Competition**: Singapore competes directly with Hong Kong for international corporate headquarters, family offices, and top talent. The rapid rise of Mainland cities like Shanghai and Shenzhen in finance and technology also poses a "catching up" challenge to Hong Kong's traditional advantages. Hong Kong must find new differentiated competitive advantages while consolidating its unique functions.
    

### 1.4 Major Policies and Strategic Responses

Faced with challenges, the HKSAR Government, with the support of the Central Government, has introduced a series of significant policies and strategies.

- **Fully Aligning with and Integrating into National Development**: This has been the core thread of Hong Kong's economic policy over the past five years.
    
    - **Guangdong-Hong Kong-Macao Greater Bay Area (GBA) Development**: Hong Kong has participated more actively in GBA construction, making progress in financial connectivity, building the I&T corridor, and aligning rules and mechanisms. Major cooperation platforms like Qianhai, Nanshan, and Hetao provide Hong Kong enterprises and professionals with a vast Mainland market hinterland.
    - **Support from the 14th Five-Year Plan**: The National 14th Five-Year Plan explicitly supports Hong Kong in consolidating and enhancing its status as an international financial, shipping, and trade center; strengthening its functions as a global offshore RMB business hub, an international asset management center, and a risk management center; and supporting Hong Kong's development into an international I&T center and a regional intellectual property trading center. This provides Hong Kong with a clear national strategic positioning and support.
- **Actively Attracting Enterprises and Talent**: To address talent drain and business relocation, the government has launched proactive measures such as the "Top Talent Pass Scheme," the new "Capital Investment Entrant Scheme," and various sector-specific funds and subsidy schemes. The results are gradually becoming evident.
    
- **Long-term Planning and Infrastructure Investment**: Promoting the "Northern Metropolis" Development Strategy as a new engine for future economic growth, aiming for deep integration with Shenzhen and other GBA Mainland cities to develop I&T and other emerging industries. Simultaneously, continuing to advance large-scale infrastructure projects like the Three-Runway System and "Lantau Tomorrow Vision" to expand economic capacity and enhance long-term competitiveness.
    
- **Maintaining the Linked Exchange Rate System and Financial Stability**: Facing severe global financial market volatility and capital outflow pressures, the Hong Kong Monetary Authority has successfully defended the stability of the Linked Exchange Rate System. The banking system's ample capital and liquidity have demonstrated the resilience of Hong Kong's financial system and maintained the confidence of international investors.

## Part II: Future Economic Development Outlook (2026-2030)

### 2.1 Growth Drivers and Potential Pathways

Looking ahead to 2026-2030, Hong Kong's economy is expected to navigate a complex environment filled with both opportunities and challenges. Growth drivers are likely to become more diversified and endogenous.

- **Financial Deepening and Functional Upgrading**: Finance will remain the core engine, but the growth model will shift from scale expansion to functional deepening and product innovation.
    
    - **Core Hub for RMB Internationalization**: As the RMB's share in international trade and reserves gradually increases, Hong Kong's central role in offshore RMB pricing, clearing, and product innovation will become even more critical. More RMB-denominated financial products (e.g., stocks, commodities, derivatives) will emerge.
    - **Green and Sustainable Finance Center**: The global energy transition and ESG investment wave will drive the continuous expansion of Hong Kong's green finance market, with the potential to become a regional and global center for green capital allocation.
    - **High Ground for Asset and Wealth Management**: Hong Kong's asset management scale is expected to return to a growth trajectory and reach new heights by 2030. Family offices, cross-border pension investment, and compliant virtual asset management will be major growth points.
    - **Risk Management Center**: Geopolitical and economic volatility will increase demand for hedging and risk management, presenting significant development space for Hong Kong in insurance, reinsurance, and various derivatives markets.
- **Industrialization Breakthroughs in I&T**: After five years of seeding and cultivation, the next five years may see industrialization gains in some fields.
    
    - **Life and Health Technology Cluster**: Leveraging the scientific research strength of top universities and attracted leading enterprises, Hong Kong has the potential to form an internationally influential industrial cluster in biotech R&D, clinical trials, and high-end medical devices.
    - **AI and Smart City Applications**: AI technology will be more widely applied in fintech, smart city management, and professional services (e.g., legal tech, construction tech), enhancing overall economic efficiency and quality.
    - **New Industrialization**: "Re-industrialization" will focus more on high-value-added, technology-intensive smart manufacturing, such as food tech, advanced materials, and precision engineering, rather than traditional labor-intensive manufacturing.
- **Northern Metropolis as a New Economic Geographic Pivot**: The Northern Metropolis will transition from planning to large-scale construction and industrial introduction. It will not just be a new residential town but will become Hong Kong's "bridgehead" for integrating into the national innovation system and collaborating with Shenzhen. The Hetao Co-operation Zone will achieve deep "One Zone, Two Parks" synergy, piloting institutional innovations in cross-boundary flows of people, goods, capital, and data, attracting world-leading tech enterprises and R&D institutions. The new development areas will create a large number of high-quality jobs and optimize Hong Kong's economic geography.
    
- **Export of High Value-Added Professional Services**: With its common law system, internationalized professionals, and unique connectivity advantage with the Mainland, Hong Kong's professional services in law, arbitration, accounting, architecture, design, and brand management will gain broader markets as Chinese enterprises "go global" and Belt and Road projects deepen, shifting from serving locally to serving regionally and globally.
    
- **Quality Upgrading of Consumption and Tourism**: Tourism will shift from pursuing visitor numbers to pursuing quality and comprehensive consumption contribution. The share of cultural, sports, and mega-event tourism will increase. Retail will further transform towards experiential, personalized, and high-end offerings. Hong Kong will strive to become a "center for cultural exchange between East and West," attracting high-spending, in-depth tourists.
    

### 2.2 Major Risks and Challenges

The path for the next five years is not without obstacles. The following risks may constrain economic development:

- **Global Economic and Geopolitical Risks**: The "high interest rate, low growth" new normal of the global economy may persist, suppressing external demand. If the risk of "decoupling and disconnection" between China and the U.S. in technology and finance intensifies, it will directly impact Hong Kong's function as a super-connector. Geopolitical hotspots like the Taiwan Strait and the South China Sea, if they heat up, could severely打击 investor confidence and regional stability.
    
- **Financial Market Volatility and Competition**: Increased volatility in global capital flows could affect Hong Kong's stock and property markets. Competition from other financial centers like Singapore, Shanghai, and Dubai will remain intense. Hong Kong must stay ahead in innovation and efficiency.
    
- **Pain of Industrial Transformation and Talent Bottlenecks**: Traditional industries (e.g., some retail, low-end services) may face contraction during economic transformation, requiring careful handling of retraining and job transitions for workers. Despite talent schemes, Hong Kong faces fierce global competition for top scientists, engineers, and fintech talent. Whether locally educated STEM talent is sufficient and willing to join local industries is also a major test.
    
- **Social Cohesion and Business Costs**: If high property prices and living costs are not alleviated over the long term, they will continue to erode social cohesion and urban vitality. How to effectively improve people's livelihoods while developing the economy is a significant governance challenge for the HKSAR government.
    
- **Data Cross-boundary Flows and Cybersecurity**: In developing the digital economy and I&T, how to achieve orderly cross-boundary flow of data elements within the GBA and even internationally, while ensuring data security and complying with national regulations, is an urgent institutional puzzle to solve.
    

### 2.3 Suggested Strategic Development Pathways

To achieve sustainable and high-quality economic development, Hong Kong needs to adopt the following strategic pathways:

- **Adhere to the Precise Positioning of "National Needs, Hong Kong's Strengths"**: Embed more deeply into the national innovation system and supply chains, playing an irreplaceable "firewall" and "testing ground" role in areas like financial opening-up and technological self-reliance. Fully utilize the GBA as economic depth and hinterland.
    
- **Promote a Better Combination of a "Proactive Government" and an "Efficient Market"**: The government needs to show greater determination and courage to reform in key areas such as land, housing, and talent. Simultaneously, through policy guidance and public resource investment, catalyze market forces to invest in emerging sectors like I&T and the green economy, reducing corporate innovation risks.
    
- **Focus on Building a Global Hub for High-End Talent**: Not only attract talent but also retain and utilize it well. This requires offering internationally competitive remuneration, a superior research environment, convenient immigration and residence policies, and high-quality education, healthcare, and living amenities.
    
- **Strengthen Regional Synergy and Rule Alignment**: Under the GBA framework, actively promote "soft connectivity" such as mutual recognition of qualifications, alignment of standards, and customs clearance facilitation, truly achieving convenient flow of production factors and optimal resource allocation, turning institutional differences into synergistic advantages.
    
- **Embrace Green and Digital Transformation**: Closely integrate carbon neutrality goals with economic transformation, vigorously develop green tech and green finance, and promote the green upgrading of traditional industries. Simultaneously, comprehensively advance the digital transformation of government, the economy, and society, building a smart city to enhance overall competitiveness.

## Conclusion

Reviewing 2021-2025, Hong Kong's economy has moved forward amidst storms. Despite setbacks, it ultimately achieved recovery and stabilization凭借 its solid institutional foundations, flexible market adaptability, and strong national support. These five years have been a period of painful adjustment and re-orientation for Hong Kong's economic structure, with traditional pillars consolidating functions under pressure and emerging sectors sprouting under policy drive.

Looking ahead to 2026-2030, the future trajectory of Hong Kong's economy will depend on its ability to successfully navigate several key relationships: finding the optimal combination between "**One Country**" and "**Two Systems**"; achieving a balance between **consolidating traditional strengths** and **cultivating new growth points**; making wise choices between **deep integration into national development** and **maintaining international characteristics**; and achieving coordination between **pursuing economic growth** and **promoting social equity**.

History shows that the greatest trait of Hong Kong's economy lies in its resilience and adaptability. As long as it can fully leverage the unique advantage of "backed by the motherland, connected to the world," persist in reform and innovation, and effectively address internal and external challenges, Hong Kong is fully capable of consolidating its international center status in the next five years and beyond, playing an even more brilliant role in the national development blueprint, and achieving long-term prosperity and stability. This process is not only about Hong Kong's own destiny but will also have a profound impact on the country's overall high-level opening-up and high-quality development.
